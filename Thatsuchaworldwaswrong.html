That such a world was wrong we knew, nothing living ever grew to wit, a wasted life was lost as by a tempest we were tossed our pale flesh pressed against the dust, so iron creatures do succumb to rust and flight a dream and we now wake to falling long to feel bones break beneath, on land so grey and desolate long years given over to regret this wan pair ruined by the hope that some strong hand held fast the end of rope to pull these poor souls free from the abyss now all consolation laden in the kiss I place upon your brow in anguished flinch that I would wake you with a playful pinch from years of misery, the toll you paid, though you did not beseech God to be made and yet through all the darkness, there a glow from which a flame I promise to see grow
