As old as we are able to imagine, worked over and over again, expanded, abstracted, turned into verse, retold in prose, translated into medieval and modern vernaculars and retranslated back into the original forgotten tongue. The prelude identifies an octogenarian monk as author. It remains unclear if he is himself a real historical person or literary invention, perhaps his own. His name, now lost, was certainly fictitious. The title found in one old manuscript: the little story book. From where, for everlasting, gleams the garden, in shadow and in breeze, as lightning fire. Black, as wind, with dry delirious heat, inflames my reason, and in the centre of my soul keeps empire for a child. We should remind ourselves, the original is lost. Recall the wise conduct of life. What is learning whose attainment sees no passion wane? Finds no congenial path, no final goal? Straining for a name, and gaining none? For the benefit of ignorant princes? The narrative illustrates, operates in succession, stories embedded, one opening within another, unending return to renewed beginning.
