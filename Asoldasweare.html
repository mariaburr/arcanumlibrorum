As old as we are able to imagine, worked over and over again, expanded, abstracted, turned into verse, retold in prose, translated into medieval and modern vernaculars and retranslated back into the original forgotten tongue. The prelude identifies an octogenarian monk as author. It remains unclear if he is himself a real historical person or literary invention, perhaps his own. His name, now lost, was certainly fictitious. The title found in one old manuscript: the little story book.
